Acoustic Welding Analysis - Semester Thesis

This project focuses on the analysis and refinement of acoustic welding processes, leveraging advanced data-driven techniques to enhance accuracy and consistency. Various methodologies were employed to process and analyze profilometry data, including height profile flattening, noise reduction, and structural evaluation. By integrating computational techniques such as median filtering, polynomial surface fitting, and Gaussian smoothing, the study aims to improve the interpretation of welding outcomes and provide insights into material behavior. The results contribute to optimizing welding parameters, ensuring high-quality joint formation, and advancing automated quality control in manufacturing applications.

Machine Learning Analysis

This section details the machine learning approach used to analyze the acoustic welding data. The analysis involved classifying the data, performing feature engineering, and applying various machine learning algorithms to extract meaningful insights. Given the limited dataset of approximately 784 samples, careful model selection was necessary to avoid overfitting. As a result, larger neural networks and other high-capacity models were not considered, ensuring a balanced approach between model complexity and generalization.
